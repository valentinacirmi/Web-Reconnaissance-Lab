# Banco Credicoop OSINT Assessment

## Objective

Conduct an Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT) assessment of Banco Credicoop using publicly available information to identify infrastructure details, exposed organizational information and potential security risks.

## Scope

Target:

* bancocredicoop.coop

Assessment Type:

* Passive reconnaissance
* Public information gathering
* Exposure analysis
* Risk identification

## Sources Used

* WHOIS
* DNS Records
* Public Search Engines
* LinkedIn
* HaveIBeenPwned
* Open Port Intelligence Sources

## Key Findings

### Domain and Infrastructure

* Active corporate domain identified.
* Multiple public IP addresses associated with the organization.
* Public network range identified.
* Dedicated DNS infrastructure detected.
* Mail protection services provided by Trend Micro.

### Technology Indicators

Public records suggest the use of:

* Google Workspace
* Atlassian Services
* Linux-based systems
* Enterprise infrastructure management tools

### Organizational Exposure

Publicly available information allowed the identification of:

* Technical administrators
* Network management personnel
* Security-related roles
* Physical office locations

### Previously Exposed Information

Public breach intelligence sources revealed historical exposure affecting organizational personnel.

Although this does not imply a current compromise, such information may increase the effectiveness of social engineering attacks.

## Security Considerations

### Information Disclosure

Publicly available technical and organizational information may assist attackers during the reconnaissance phase.

Examples include:

* Infrastructure details
* Administrative contacts
* Technology stack indicators
* Public network ranges

### Social Engineering Risks

Identified personnel in security and infrastructure roles may become potential targets for:

* Spear phishing
* Credential harvesting
* Business email compromise
* Impersonation attacks

### Infrastructure Intelligence

Information regarding server management tools, operating systems and automation platforms may provide valuable intelligence for adversaries attempting to profile the environment.

## Conclusion

The assessment demonstrates how publicly available information can reveal valuable details about an organization's infrastructure, technologies and personnel.

While no active exploitation was performed, the collected information highlights the importance of minimizing unnecessary exposure and maintaining strong security awareness practices.

## Disclaimer

This project was conducted exclusively for educational purposes as part of an Ethical Hacking training program.

All information was obtained from publicly available sources. No exploitation, unauthorized access or modification of target systems was performed.
